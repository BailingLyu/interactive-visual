Population Distribution
The entire population from which we draw samples. This distribution has a fixed mean (μ) and standard deviation (σ).
Current Sample Distribution
The distribution of observations in the most recently drawn sample. The sample mean (x̄) varies from sample to sample.
Sampling Distribution of Means
The distribution of sample means (x̄) from repeated sampling. Watch it build as you draw more samples. Notice how it centers on μ and becomes narrower as n increases.

Key Insights:

Central Limit Theorem: As you increase the sample size (n), the sampling distribution of means becomes more normal and less variable. The standard error (SE = σ/√n) decreases, meaning sample means cluster more tightly around the population mean.
